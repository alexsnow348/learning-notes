# BBC In Our Time Daily Episode Summary

## Episode: John Keats
**Date:** February 19, 2026  
**Focus:** The short, brilliant life and intensely creative year of one of England's greatest Romantic poets

### Key Points

**Context and Background:**
- John Keats (1795-1821) produced some of the most beloved poems in English during his most productive year
- His creative peak began in autumn 1818, just two years after leaving his work as an apothecary to pursue poetry
- Keats died in Rome from tuberculosis at age 25, before his work achieved fame

**The Critical Year (Autumn 1818):**
- During this period, Keats wrote some of his most famous works including:
  - *Ode to a Nightingale*
  - *Ode on a Grecian Urn*  
  - *Ode on Melancholy*
- This creative burst came despite harsh criticism from reviewers who called him an "uncouth Cockney" who should return to his former work

**Expert Panel:**
- Fiona Stafford: Professor of English Language and Literature at Oxford University
- Nicholas Roe: Wardlaw Professor of English Literature at University of St Andrews
- Meiko O'Halloran: Senior Lecturer in Romantic Literature at Newcastle University

**Producer:** Simon Tillotson

**Reading List:**
- Nicholas Roe, *John Keats: A New Life* (Yale University Press, 2012)
- Lucasta Miller, *Keats: A Brief Life in Nine Poems and One Epitaph* (Jonathan Cape, 2021)
- Susan J. Wolfson, *Reading John Keats* (Cambridge University Press, 2015)
- Multiple editions of Keats' complete poems and selected writings

**Episode Duration:** 48 minutes
**Broadcast Time:** Sunday 23:00 on BBC Radio 4

### Themes Explored:
- The relationship between artistic criticism and creative output
- The intensity of Romantic poetry and its enduring legacy
- The tragic brevity of Keats' life contrasted with the timelessness of his work
- How personal hardship and illness influenced his poetic vision

### Historical Context:
- Keats lived during a period of significant social and artistic change
- His work challenged established literary norms and social hierarchies
- The episode explores how his Cockney background influenced both his reception and his poetry

### Legacy:
- Despite dying young and receiving harsh criticism during his lifetime
- Keats' poetry has become some of the most cherished in the English language
- His odes are considered masterpieces of Romantic literature
- The episode examines why his work continues to resonate nearly 200 years after his death
