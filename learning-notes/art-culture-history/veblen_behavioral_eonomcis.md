- Conspicuous Consumption: Spending driven by the desire to be seen spending; value comes from visibility, not utility.
- Conspicuous Leisure: Displaying freedom from work through curated downtime, travel, and “slow living” aesthetics.
- Pecuniary Emulation: Imitating the consumption of higher-status groups; the engine of aspirational lifestyles.
- Social Signaling: Communicating identity through choices—luxury, minimalism, eco-branding, or digital assets.
- Heuristics & Biases: Mental shortcuts and predictable misjudgments that shape real-world economic behavior.
- Identity Economics: Consumption choices shaped by who we believe ourselves to be, and the groups we belong to.
- Algorithmic Comparison Loop: A digital feedback cycle—feeds, likes, follows—that intensifies constant status comparison.
- Veblen Goods: Items whose desirability increases with price because scarcity and expense are the point.
- Conspicuous Intelligence: Using technology, knowledge, or tools (AI agents, GPUs, custom models) to signal expertise.
